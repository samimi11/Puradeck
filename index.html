The problem

Singapore consumers trust AU/NZ/CA wellness brands — but discovery remains fragmented.

01

Mass assortment

Pharmacies and mass retailers prioritize breadth and promotions over clean-label brand storytelling.

02

Marketplace noise

Shopee and Lazada offer volume, but trust is uneven and counterfeit concerns shape premium buying behavior.

03

No provenance lens

Origin-specific brands from AU, NZ, and Canada are sought after, yet rarely assembled in a single curated journey.

04

Routine gap

Consumers want wellness routines, bundles, and education — not just a single-SKU marketplace transaction.

What this shows

All existing players cluster toward mass market and generic assortment. The curated + origin-specific quadrant is effectively unoccupied — that is where Pura is building.

  • 📦 Shopee / iHerb: large selection, low curation, generic global browsing
  • 💊 Watsons / Guardian: trusted retail, mass-market and promo-led
  • 🌿 AHW: closer to premium, but not origin-focused by design
  • ✦ Pura: purpose-built for AU / NZ / CA provenance + high curation

Business Model6 / 12
Go-to-market

Three disciplined phases — launch around proof, then scale around retention.

Phase 1 — Launch

Founder-led curation, KOL seeding in wellness and clean beauty, WhatsApp concierge channel, and content-native paid social targeting Singapore’s 68,000+ expat and premium-local consumer segments.

Phase 2 — Retention

Introduce wellness bundles, monthly subscriptions, referral loops, and routine-based CRM segmentation to improve cohort retention and lift lifetime value per customer.

Phase 3 — Scale

Exclusive brand partnerships, offline activations and pop-ups, and measured expansion into adjacent SEA urban centers — Kuala Lumpur, Bangkok, and Jakarta.
